JAIN UNIVERSITY FET ANTI-RAGGING POLICY

ANTI-RAGGING STATEMENT
Ragging is prohibited in Jain (Deemed-to-be University) FET Campus.

WHAT COUNTS AS RAGGING
Any disorderly conduct in any manner, whether by verbal or written communication or by any act that has the effect of teasing or handling any student with rudeness, falls under ragging.
Such interaction can be brutal, barbaric, and anti-social and is treated strictly under the law.
Students indulging in undisciplined activities that cause or are likely to cause annoyance, hardship, psychological harm, physical harm, or fear in a junior or fresher student will not be tolerated.
Compelling a student to perform any action that causes shame or embarrassment is strictly prohibited.

ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY
- Ragging is strictly prohibited within the Jain University campus, including institutions, departments, and hostels.
- Ragging in any form is banned in private hostels or buildings where students are staying.
- No person, including students, staff, or faculty, shall participate in or propagate ragging in any form.

POSSIBLE PUNISHMENTS
The university may take strict action against anyone found guilty of ragging.
Punishments may include:
- Expulsion from the institution
- Cancellation of admission
- Suspension or rustication
- Expulsion from the university or hostel
- Debarring the student from admission to any other educational institution for a definite period

COMPLAINT SUPPORT
If any student is facing ragging or has been ragged, the student should complain to the Dean of Student Affairs.

QUICK ANSWERS
Is ragging allowed in Jain University FET campus: No, ragging is strictly prohibited
Does the anti-ragging policy apply to hostels: Yes, it applies to campus hostels and even private hostels where students stay
Who should a student contact if facing ragging: The Dean of Student Affairs
What actions can be taken against ragging: Suspension, cancellation of admission, expulsion, rustication, hostel expulsion, and debarring from future admission
