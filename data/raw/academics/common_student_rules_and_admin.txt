ACADEMIC RULES
Attendance Requirement: 85% minimum per course
Assignment Submission Rules: Assignments must be submitted before the deadline.
Exam and Evaluation Pattern:
- Theory: 50% CA + 50% ESE
- Practical: 70% CA + 30% ESE
Eligibility for End Semester Exam: Minimum 85% attendance
Promotion and Progression Rules: Minimum SGPA 4.0 to pass

STUDENT GUIDELINES
Dress Code:
- Boys: No bermudas or shorts, torn jeans, or ear studs. Hair should be short and trimmed.
- Girls: No transparent, short, sleeveless outfits, or three-fourth pants.
- Avoid colourful hair dye.
- Wear formal footwear.
- No body piercings or tattoos.
College Timings: 08:45 AM to 03:45 PM with a 50-minute lunch break
ID Card Requirement: Wearing ID cards inside the premises is mandatory.
Mobile Phone Rule: Cell phone usage inside classrooms and laboratories is not permitted.
Discipline Rules: Violations of the code may result in warnings, suspension, or expulsion.
Anti-Ragging Rule: Ragging is a crime punishable by law.
Leave of Absence Rule: Prior permission is required for leave due to ill health, emergencies, or important family events.
Medical Certificate Rule: Submit medical certificates within one day of returning to college, with the class teacher's signature.

FEES AND ADMINISTRATION
Fee Payment Rule: All required fees must be paid on time as stipulated by the institute or hostel.
Fee Refund Policy: No fees will be refunded after completing the enrolment process.
Approval Requirements:
- Prior permission from the Program Head, HoD, or Director is required for competitions and fests.
- Prior permission is required for leave when applicable.
Important Contacts:
- Prof. Samiran Bhattacharyya, Deputy Dean (Student Affairs) - for further fee-related information

IMPORTANT NOTES
- Students must attend college from the first day.
- Students must regularly check the LMS for syllabus updates, assignments, notes, and other academic information.
- Participation in co-curricular and extra-curricular activities is mandatory when required by the college authorities.
- Students must maintain a minimum of 85% attendance in every course to be eligible for the end-semester university examination.
- False information in applications or documents can lead to admission cancellation, withholding of hall tickets or results, and legal action.
- Delayed fee transit is not accepted as a reason for late payment.
- Failure to pay fees on time can lead to removal from rolls and restriction from classes, hostel, course registration, and examinations.
- Students must attend all classes and labs, including online classes, regularly.
- Smoking, alcohol, tobacco use, and narcotic substances on campus are punishable and may lead to suspension, debarment, dismissal, or expulsion.
- Non-vegetarian food and pets are not allowed on campus or in campus hostels.
- Students must respond to study-related university communications within 24 hours.
- The university recommends avoiding private vehicle use; unauthorized parking may lead to vehicle seizure.
- Repeated disciplinary violations may lead to expulsion from the institute.
